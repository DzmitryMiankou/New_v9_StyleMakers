Minimalism in painting is an art movement characterized by extreme simplicity, precision, and a focus on essential elements. Here are some key characteristics: Simplicity of Form:

Approach: Minimalists emphasize literal, objective representation. Their works do not refer to anything beyond themselves. The focus is on the immediate visual experience.

Anonymity and Reduction: Factory-made and shop-bought materials are favored, downplaying the artist’s hand. Minimalism seeks to eliminate extraneous elements, allowing viewers to engage directly with the artwork.
